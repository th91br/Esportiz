# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Esportiz is used by owners, managers, reception teams, instructors, finance operators, and students of sports schools and arenas. The primary context is daily operation: checking schedules, managing students or reservants, receiving payments, reviewing reports, and keeping the business organized from desktop or mobile, including PWA use near the court.

## Product Purpose

Esportiz centralizes the operational and financial management of sports schools and arenas in a responsive SaaS product. Success means the user can understand the current state of the business quickly, act with confidence, and complete frequent workflows without friction or ambiguity.

## Brand Personality

Clear, premium, professional, secure, and practical. The interface should feel like an intelligent operating system for the business: polished enough to earn trust, but restrained enough that managers and staff can move fast.

## Anti-references

Esportiz should not look like a generic SaaS dashboard, an overdecorated dark interface with excessive glow, an old bureaucratic admin panel, or a visual experiment that slows routine work. Decorative effects, vague marketing polish, and inconsistent component styles should be avoided when they compete with clarity.

## Design Principles

1. Clarity before decoration: every screen should make the next operational action obvious.
2. Premium through restraint: use spacing, alignment, contrast, typography, and consistency before visual effects.
3. Trust through predictable patterns: repeated workflows should use the same button, form, card, and state vocabulary across modules.
4. Mobile is operational, not secondary: responsive layouts should keep actions reachable and data readable on small screens.
5. Secure by default: private data, errors, destructive actions, permissions, and loading states should communicate confidence without exposing unnecessary detail.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, keyboard focus, touch targets, semantic labels, and readable mobile layouts. Support reduced motion, avoid relying on color alone for status, and keep text legible for users under time pressure or using the app in bright outdoor environments.
